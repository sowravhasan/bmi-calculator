Frequently Asked Questions

BMI is a simple calculation that uses height and weight to estimate whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. It's widely used by healthcare professionals as a screening tool to identify potential weight-related health risks.
The formula is: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ [height (m)]². For example, a person weighing 70 kg and 1.75 m tall has a BMI of 22.9 (healthy range). Our calculator does this automatically when you enter your height in centimeters and weight in kilograms.
According to the World Health Organization (WHO):
  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5
  • Normal weight: 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: 25 – 29.9
  • Obese: BMI ≥ 30
BMI is a useful screening tool but does not account for muscle mass, age, or body composition. Athletes with high muscle mass may have a high BMI but low body fat. Similarly, older adults may have a normal BMI but higher body fat percentage. Consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.
Monitoring BMI helps track your health and can alert you to potential risks like diabetes, heart disease, and obesity. Regular BMI checks can help you maintain a healthy weight, set fitness goals, and make informed decisions about your lifestyle and diet.

Important Disclaimer

This BMI calculator provides general information only and should not be taken as medical advice. BMI is a screening tool and doesn't diagnose body fatness or health. For personalized guidance and professional health assessment, please consult a qualified healthcare provider.
